01Acceptance of terms

By creating an account, booking a session, or just browsing tutorhouse.com, you agree to these terms. If you don't agree, that's fair — but please don't use the service. These terms form a legal agreement between you and Tutor House Inc., a Delaware corporation headquartered in Brooklyn, NY.

You'll need to be at least 13 to use Tutor House. If you're under 18, a parent or legal guardian needs to agree to these terms on your behalf and will be responsible for your activity on the platform.

02Your account

You're responsible for keeping your login credentials secret and for everything that happens on your account. Pick a real password. Tell us right away at security@tutorhouse.com if you think someone else got in.

One human, one account. Don't share, sell, or transfer your account. We may suspend accounts that look automated or abusive.

03Booking sessions

Tutors set their own availability and rates. When you book, you and the tutor enter a direct service agreement; Tutor House facilitates the introduction, scheduling, and payment but is not the tutor.

Cancellations

  • You can reschedule or cancel for free up to 12 hours before a session starts.
  • Inside 12 hours, the tutor may charge up to 50% of the session fee.
  • No-shows (15+ minutes late with no contact) count as a completed session.

04Payments & refunds

Payment is taken when you book and held until 24 hours after the session, after which it's released to your tutor. Tutor House does not charge a platform fee to learners — what you see at checkout is what you pay.

First-session promise. If your first paid session with any new tutor doesn't click, we refund it in full — no forms, no calls. Just reply to your booking email within 7 days and say "first-session refund."

Disputes

If something goes wrong in a session — the tutor didn't show, was unprepared, or behaved unprofessionally — open a dispute within 14 days of the session. We review every dispute personally; most are resolved within 48 hours.

05If you're a tutor

You're an independent contractor, not an employee of Tutor House. You set your rates, choose your hours, and decide which students to take on.

  • You agree to deliver sessions in good faith — prepared, on time, and professional.
  • You won't take students off-platform to avoid paying out (anti-circumvention is a serious term — see section 9).
  • You're responsible for your own taxes. We issue 1099s in the US for tutors earning $600+ per year.
  • You grant us a non-exclusive license to display your profile, photo, and outcome data on the platform.

06Acceptable use

Don't:

  • Harass, threaten, or discriminate against anyone — students, tutors, or our staff.
  • Use the platform to cheat on graded assignments, exams, or admissions essays. We're a learning service, not a homework-completion service.
  • Scrape, reverse-engineer, or build derivative services from our platform without written permission.
  • Impersonate anyone, misrepresent credentials, or post fake reviews.
  • Upload malware, attempt unauthorized access, or otherwise mess with the service.

Violations get warned once, then suspended. Egregious violations (harassment, fraud, abuse of minors) skip the warning.

07Your content

You own what you upload — notes, recordings, profile photos, reviews. By uploading, you grant Tutor House a worldwide, royalty-free license to host, display, and distribute that content as needed to operate the service.

Session recordings, when enabled by both parties, are stored encrypted and retained for 90 days unless you choose to keep them longer in your library.

08Cookies

We use a small set of cookies to keep you logged in, remember preferences, and understand which features get used. You can opt out of analytics cookies in your account settings — that won't affect the core service.

We don't use third-party advertising cookies. We don't sell behavioral data. Full breakdown of what we collect and why lives in our privacy policy.

09Liability

The service is provided "as is." We work hard to keep it reliable but can't guarantee it'll be available 100% of the time or that any specific tutor will get you a specific outcome.

Our maximum liability to you, in any 12-month period, is capped at the greater of (a) $250 or (b) the fees you paid us in that period. This doesn't apply to losses we can't legally cap (gross negligence, willful misconduct, etc.).

10Changes & contact

We may update these terms — for clarity, new features, or legal reasons. Material changes get emailed to you 30 days before they take effect. If you don't agree to a change, close your account before it takes effect.

Questions, complaints, or compliance requests: legal@tutorhouse.com or 220 Atlantic Ave, Suite 300, Brooklyn NY 11201.
