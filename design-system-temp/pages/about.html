We make great teachers findable.

Tutor House started in 2019 with a simple frustration: the best tutors were buried under spam, and the worst ones had the loudest profiles. So we built the platform we wished existed — vetted, transparent, and ruthlessly student-first.

Brooklyn HQ · est. 2019
Our story

Built by educators, for the students they couldn't reach.

Our founders — Maya, a high-school math teacher, and Jordan, an ed-tech engineer — met in a Brooklyn coffee shop in 2018. Maya had spent the year referring her students to tutors she trusted. Jordan had spent it watching marketplace platforms reward whoever paid the most for ads.

"Every kid deserves the tutor I'd send my own to. Not the one with the slickest profile."

That mismatch became Tutor House. We hand-vet every tutor through a three-stage interview, publish honest outcome data, and refund any first session that doesn't click. No ad slots. No premium placements. The marketplace ranks on results.

Six years later, we're a remote team of 38 across Brooklyn, Lagos, and Dhaka — half educators, half engineers — serving 94,000+ learners across 47 countries.

What we believe

Five rules we won't compromise on.

01 / Vetted

Quality over catalog size.

We'd rather have 2,400 great tutors than 24,000 anyone-can-join profiles. Every applicant runs the gauntlet — subject test, mock session, reference call.

02 / Transparent

Outcomes, in public.

Every tutor profile shows real outcome data: average grade lift, completion rate, repeat-booking rate. No padded stats, no fake reviews.

03 / Aligned

No paid placements, ever.

Tutors can't pay to rank higher. Search results sort by fit and outcomes — full stop. Our incentives stay pointed at the learner.

04 / Forgiving

First session is on us.

If your first session doesn't click, we refund it. No forms, no calls, no "how can we keep your business?" Just the money back.

05 / Human

Real people, real replies.

Support is staffed by former teachers — not ticket-deflecting bots. Median first-reply time during business hours: under 4 minutes.

06 / Honest

We say no a lot.

Tutoring isn't right for every problem. If you'd be better served by a free YouTube playlist, we'll tell you and link to one. Trust compounds.
